Data Science: First Dive

I like to travel, like a lot. As a kid, I would dream about not simply visiting all the world countries but living there, experiencing their culture, and learning the local language. So I would give myself names such as "World Walker" or "Globe trotter." The undying desire to know the planet spur my curiosity in all subjects. As an adult, I understood, among other challenging aspects, the potential dangers of this endeavor. In recent years, I became interested in the spread of terrorism across the globe. My search led me to the Global Terrorism Database (https://start.umd.edu/gtd/), which provides information on more than 200,000 terrorist attacks. Using python, I decided to explore the data and acquire some insight on the topic.

Presented here is my first complete data science pipeline project. It includes data-wrangling and exploration, data visualization, and basic regression. The project was a fantastic insight into how terrorist activities have evolved and spread around the globe. For instance, in 1970, the US had the highest number of attacks, but in 2018 the Middle East, North Africa, and South Asia saw more activities. Between 1970 and 2018, Zimbabwe has the most attack counts, with over 2000 attacks reported, most of them between 2010 and 2018. Conversely, Afghanistan has the lowest number of attacks, with only one attack reported.
I also learned several data wrangling and data exploration techniques, which will be carried to other projects.

Keywords: Python, Pandas, Folium, Sklearn, BeautifulSoup
Completed: April 2018
